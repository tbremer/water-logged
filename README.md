# Water Logged

A standalone **Apple Watch** hydration app:

- 🔔 **"Time to hydrate" reminders** on a cadence you choose (15 min, 30 min, 1 / 2 / 4 hr — default hourly), within an active-hours window (so you're not woken at 3 AM).
- 💧 **One-tap logging** in three increments: **8 oz / 16 oz / 24 oz** (also loggable straight from the notification).
- 🎯 **Daily goal** (default **96 oz**) shown as a progress ring on the Today screen.
- 📅 **Daily history** of your fluid intake, with per-day totals and swipe-to-delete.
- ☁️ **iCloud sync** via SwiftData + CloudKit (on a signed-in device).
- 🍎 Optional **Apple Health** saving — logged drinks are written as dietary water.

Built with SwiftUI + SwiftData, targeting **watchOS 10+** (developed against Xcode 26.5 / watchOS 26.5).

- **Bundle identifier:** `com.tbremer.waterlogged`
- **iCloud container:** `iCloud.com.tbremer.waterlogged`

## Quick start (simulator)

The project file is generated by [XcodeGen](https://github.com/yonsm/XcodeGen) from `project.yml`,
so generate it first, then open it.

```fish
brew install xcodegen          # one time, if you don't have it
xcodegen generate              # creates WaterLogged.xcodeproj
open WaterLogged.xcodeproj     # then press Run in Xcode
```

Or do everything (generate + build + launch in the Simulator) with the helper script:

```fish
./Scripts/run-sim.fish
# or target a specific watch:
./Scripts/run-sim.fish "Apple Watch Ultra 3 (49mm)"
```

> First launch downloads nothing extra **if** you already have a watchOS simulator runtime.
> If Xcode says *"watchOS NN is not installed"*, install the runtime once with:
> ```fish
> xcodebuild -downloadPlatform watchOS
> ```

## Installing on your Apple Watch

> **This is a standalone watch app — there is no iPhone app.** Xcode installs it **directly onto
> your Apple Watch**. It will *not* appear as an icon on your iPhone's home screen, and there is no
> "install on the phone first" step. (It will show up in the iPhone **Watch app → Installed on Apple
> Watch** list once it's on the watch.) Your watch does need to already be paired to an iPhone, as
> all Apple Watches do.

Simulators can't fully exercise notifications, HealthKit, or iCloud, so use your real watch for real testing.

### First, your Apple Developer account matters

This app declares **iCloud (CloudKit)**, **HealthKit**, and **push** (used by CloudKit sync)
capabilities. Those are *restricted* entitlements that require a **paid Apple Developer Program**
membership (~$99/yr):

- **Paid membership** → everything works as shipped. Follow "Install (paid account)" below.
- **Free Apple ID** (personal team) → you can still side-load to your own watch, but a free account
  **cannot** provision iCloud/HealthKit/push, so the build will fail to sign until you remove them.
  See "Install (free account)". You lose iCloud sync and Apple Health saving; hourly reminders,
  logging, and history still work.

### Prepare the watch (one time)

1. Make sure the watch is paired to your iPhone (normal setup).
2. The **Developer Mode** toggle is hidden until Xcode targets the watch once. Connect your iPhone
   to the Mac and press **Run** in Xcode; when it reports *"Developer Mode disabled"*, the toggle
   appears on the watch under **Settings → Privacy & Security → Developer Mode** → turn it on →
   the watch restarts and asks you to confirm.
3. Keep your **Mac, iPhone, and watch on the same Wi-Fi**, and put the watch on its charger
   (the watch has no data cable — installs happen wirelessly and the first one can take several minutes).

### Install (paid account)

1. `xcodegen generate && open WaterLogged.xcodeproj`
2. Signing is pre-configured in `project.yml`:
   - **Team** `Z5DFW5G77C` and bundle id `com.tbremer.waterlogged` are already set. If you ever
     switch teams, update `DEVELOPMENT_TEAM` in `project.yml` and re-run `xcodegen generate`
     (don't set it only in Xcode's UI — `xcodegen generate` would overwrite it).
   - The iCloud + HealthKit capabilities are already declared; automatic signing provisions the
     `iCloud.com.tbremer.waterlogged` container for you.
3. In the Xcode toolbar destination menu, pick **your Apple Watch** (not a simulator) and press
   **Run** (⌘R).
4. If the watch warns about an untrusted developer, on the **iPhone** go to
   **Settings → General → VPN & Device Management → [your profile] → Trust**.
5. On first launch on the watch: **Allow Notifications** (required for reminders). Apple Health
   access is only requested later, if you turn on "Save to Apple Health" in Settings.

### Install (free account)

A free Apple ID can't use iCloud/HealthKit/push, so strip those first:

- In `WaterLogged/WaterLogged.entitlements`, remove the `com.apple.developer.icloud-*`,
  `aps-environment`, and `com.apple.developer.healthkit*` keys (or delete the file and remove
  `CODE_SIGN_ENTITLEMENTS` from `project.yml`).
- In `WaterLogged/Info.plist`, remove `NSHealthUpdateUsageDescription`.
- `PersistenceController` already falls back to a local-only store, so the app keeps working.

Then follow steps 1–5 above (your free "Personal Team" is fine). Notifications, logging, and history
work; iCloud sync and Apple Health saving are disabled.

## How the "only when worn & awake" requirement is handled

You asked for reminders only when the watch is actively worn and you're awake. Here's the honest
state of the platform and what this app does:

- **Wrist detection:** watchOS does **not** expose any public "is the watch on your wrist" API to
  third-party apps, and local notifications fire on their schedule regardless of wear state. There
  is no supported way to gate a reminder on wrist contact. What *does* happen for free: when the
  watch is off-wrist/locked, watchOS routes alerts to your iPhone instead, and **Sleep Focus**
  automatically suppresses interruptions overnight.
- **Awake / not sleeping (best effort):** reminders are only scheduled within your configured
  **active-hours window** (default **8 AM–10 PM**), so they won't fire overnight.

So: reminders effectively fire only during your waking window. True wrist-contact gating isn't
possible on watchOS today — this is a platform limitation, not an app bug.

## Settings

On the **Settings** screen (third page):

| Setting | Default | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Daily goal | 96 oz | 8–300 oz, in 8 oz steps |
| Reminders | On | Master on/off |
| Interval | 1 hr | 15 min, 30 min, 1 / 2 / 4 hr |
| Start / End hour | 8 AM / 10 PM | Active window |
| Save to Apple Health | Off | Writes logged water to Health |

Changing any reminder setting re-arms the schedule immediately. The schedule is also rebuilt when
the app becomes active and via a daily background refresh (~3:30 AM).

## iCloud / CloudKit notes

- Sync uses SwiftData's CloudKit integration with your app's **private** database.
- CloudKit mirroring is **enabled only on a real device that's signed into iCloud**. On the
  simulator (and when no iCloud account is present) the app uses a local-only store. This is
  deliberate: ad-hoc/simulator builds have no provisioned CloudKit container, and Core Data's
  CloudKit layer would otherwise crash asynchronously. See `PersistenceController.swift`.
- The `DrinkEntry` model is CloudKit-compatible (all properties have defaults, no unique
  constraints, no required relationships), per Apple's requirements.
- Before shipping to the App Store, **promote your CloudKit schema to Production** in the CloudKit
  Console (development schema is created automatically as you run debug builds).

## Project structure

```
project.yml                     XcodeGen spec (edit this, then `xcodegen generate`)
Scripts/run-sim.fish            Generate + build + launch on a simulator
Tools/MakeIcon.swift            Regenerates the app icon PNG
WaterLogged/
  WaterLoggedApp.swift          @main App; wires ModelContainer + scene phase
  Info.plist                    WKApplication/WKWatchOnly + Health usage string
  WaterLogged.entitlements      iCloud (CloudKit), HealthKit, aps-environment
  Models/                       DrinkEntry (SwiftData), DrinkSize (8/16/24)
  Persistence/                  ModelContainer (CloudKit + local fallback)
  Settings/                     AppSettings (@Observable, UserDefaults-backed)
  Notifications/                HydrationScheduler + AppDelegate (actions, bg refresh)
  Health/                       HydrationHealthStore (HealthKit water write, optional)
  Views/                        Today, ProgressRing, LogButtons, History, DayDetail, Settings
  Support/                      WaterLog, Formatters
  Assets.xcassets/              AppIcon (water drop), AccentColor
```

`WaterLogged.xcodeproj` is **generated** and git-ignored — re-run `xcodegen generate` after adding
or renaming files. To regenerate the icon: `swift Tools/MakeIcon.swift WaterLogged/Assets.xcassets/AppIcon.appiconset/icon-1024.png`.
